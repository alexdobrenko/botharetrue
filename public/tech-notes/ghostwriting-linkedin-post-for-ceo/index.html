Step-by-Step Tutorial#

What This Does#

Research a podcast guest’s social media presence across platforms, analyze their actual posting style, and draft LinkedIn copy that sounds like them—not like a marketing team wrote it.

The Prompts#

Research phase:

Can you research the details for [Guest] across social platforms and for [Company] across socials? And what other channels they might have - email newsletters etc

Voice analysis:

Can you search thru what [Guest] posts on his own LinkedIn to see if we have precedent for how to do this?

Drafting:

Write it now more in [Guest's] voice from the transcript of the podcast and from his prior social posts

What Claude Does#

  1. Searches LinkedIn, X, Instagram for guest + company presence
  2. Fetches actual post content to analyze length, tone, structure
  3. Identifies patterns (e.g., “he always thanks the interviewer first”)
  4. Pulls key quotes from podcast transcript
  5. Drafts post matching their actual voice
  6. Iterates based on feedback (length, angle, structure)

Example Results#

Before (generic AI voice):

Every tech revolution has sparked the same fear: machines will take our jobs. I’ve been thinking about this differently. Here’s what we discovered inside Cognizant… [480 words, essay format]

After (matched to guest’s actual style):

AI can be the great equalizer of employment opportunities. I sat down with my friend Connor Diemand-Yauman on Is This Working?! to talk about why I believe this—and what it will take to make it real. [320 words, his actual patterns]
